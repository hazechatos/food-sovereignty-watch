Food Sovereignty Watch

Exploring food self-sufficiency by country over time

In this dashboard, the self-sufficiency rate is the share of domestic availability covered by national production (approximated as production / (production + imports)). Source: FAOSTAT country-year-product data.

Poultry Chicken meat, fresh or chilled — a key protein source across Europe. Contrary to wheat and milk, chicken meat has a short production cycle - around 40 days for a standard chicken. Logistic pressure makes this product sensitive to importations. More recently, the EU-Mercosur deal is seen as a growing risk for European self-sufficiency.
Wheat The staple cereal grain — central to bread, pasta and food security. Very discriminating: auto-sufficiency drops in extreme weather like in Norway or Portugal. On top of that, countries with intense industries in wheat-processed products like pasta cannot rely solely on their own production. Take a look at Italy!
Milk Raw cattle milk — a cornerstone of the European dairy industry. Look, European countries are all good players here. But there is a reason for that. Milk is difficult to transport: it is highly perishable, and a disruption of the cold chain can induce bacteria development (Listeria, Salmonella).
